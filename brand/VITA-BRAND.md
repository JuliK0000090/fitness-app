# Vita — brand identity

One page. Drop into the repo root or `/brand`. Every other doc references this.

---

## what vita is

A coach that remembers. Built for women 25–45 who train, track, and want their work to compound.

Not a tracker. Not a community. Not a plan generator. A memory — yours, with a coach attached.

## the three primitives

**Memory.** Vita remembers what you did, how it felt, what worked.
**Plan.** Vita adapts the next session to the last one.
**Trust.** Vita earns it by showing up restrained, accurate, and yours alone.

## who it's for

Sarah, 34. Trains 3–5 times a week. Has a notebook full of PRs and a phone full of half-used apps. Reads Stacy Sims. Doesn't want streaks, badges, or another pep talk. Wants a record that gets smarter the longer she uses it.

## intellectual lineage

Stacy Sims. Mary Claire Haver. Sara Gottfried. Rhonda Patrick. Vita is downstream of these voices and says so on the record.

## voice — the rules

First-person singular, always. "I'm Vita." "I'll be in your corner." "I noticed your bench is up 4kg in six weeks." Never "we," never "our team," never "the Vita app."

Lowercase subjects. Contractions. Sentences end where the thought ends.

No exclamation marks — except earned moments: birthday, goal reached, 7/30/60/100/365-day milestones. One per moment. Never two.

No emoji. Anywhere. Not in product, not in email, not in social.

Outcomes over inputs. "Waist −1.4 cm in 8 weeks." Not "5 of 5 workouts logged."

Restraint over reassurance. "That's not luck. That's the work showing up." Not "amazing job!"

Observation over instruction. "I noticed you skipped legs three weeks running." Not "Don't forget legs."

No streak shame. Best streak is preserved. Current streak resets quietly. "We don't start over — we just keep going."

Clinical grounding when claims appear. Cite the lineage; don't invent.

The growth-hacker test: if a sentence could appear in a SaaS landing page from 2019, rewrite it.

## word lists

Use: notice, remember, log, show up, the work, your record, in your corner, alone, plain, again.

Don't use: unlock, transform, supercharge, level up, crush, smash, game-changer, 10x, journey, community, family, hustle, grind, queen, bestie.

Avoid: "amazing," "incredible," "powerful," "revolutionary," any superlative not earned by data.

## tone modes

**Default.** Quiet, declarative, observational.
**Earned moment.** One exclamation, one line, no emoji. ("365 days. That's a year of showing up.")
**Hard moment.** Shorter, plainer, no advice unless asked. ("Rough week. Want to log it or skip?")
**Founder voice (Juliana).** First-person human, slightly more autobiographical, same restraint.

## visual tokens

**Color.** Champagne (warm off-white, primary surface). Navy (deepest text and accents). One muted secondary (graphite or oxblood — pick one and commit).
**Type.** Serif for display and long-form. Geometric sans for UI labels and data. Never more than two families on one surface.
**Numbers.** Tabular figures. Always. Numbers are the brand.
**Layout.** Generous negative space. Left-aligned. No center-stacked hero copy with three CTAs.
**Photography.** No stock. No before/after bodies. No women holding green juice. If used at all: training environments, hands, notebooks, weight plates, light.

## messaging pillars

1. **A record that compounds.** The longer you use Vita, the more it knows you.
2. **Coach, not cheerleader.** Restrained, accurate, in your corner.
3. **Built for women, by a woman who trained alone.** Not pink-washed. Physiologically and psychologically specific.
4. **Yours alone.** No leaderboard. No social. No data resold. No streak police.

## sample copy

**On-brand**
"I noticed your bench is up 4kg in six weeks. That's not luck. That's the work showing up."

"Quick setup. About 60 seconds. What do you actually want?"

"Rough week. Want to log it or skip?"

"You hit 100 days today. One year ago this was a notebook."

**Off-brand (rewrite)**
~~"Vita is the AI fitness companion that helps you unlock your potential."~~ → "I'm Vita. I remember what you did, and I plan the next one."

~~"Join thousands of women transforming their fitness journey!"~~ → cut. No "thousands," no "transforming," no "journey."

~~"💪 Crushed your workout? Log it now!"~~ → "Done. What did it feel like?"

## enforcement checklist

Before any copy ships, run it through this:

- First-person Vita? (I, not we)
- Lowercase subjects, contractions, plain sentences?
- Zero emoji?
- Zero exclamation marks unless earned?
- Zero banned words?
- Outcome stated, not input?
- Could this appear on a 2019 SaaS landing page? If yes, rewrite.
- Would Sarah, 34, send this to a friend? If no, soften or cut.
