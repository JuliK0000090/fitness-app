# Vita — 90-Day Launch Campaign Plan

Read alongside [LAUNCH_CHECKLIST.md](LAUNCH_CHECKLIST.md) (technical
readiness), [brand/VITA-brand/VITA-BRAND.md](brand/VITA-brand/VITA-BRAND.md) (voice), and the competitive brief in
the conversation log. All copy in this plan must pass the
eight-point checklist in brand/VITA-BRAND.md.

**Plan window:** Day 1–90, beginning the day after the launch checklist
goes fully green.

**Operating constraint:** founder-led. One person, finite hours. The
plan assumes no agency, no full-time marketer, and a paid budget that
only turns on if Phase 2 content earns organic traction.

**The angle to own (from competitive brief):** *Vita is the trainer who
compounds.* Memory is the moat. Wearables are proof. Outcomes are the
receipt.

---

## Objectives

Three tiers per metric so the plan stays honest at every velocity.

| Metric | Floor (must-hit) | Target (likely with execution) | Stretch (best case) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trial starts (cumulative, 90d) | 250 | 1,000 | 2,500 |
| Paid conversions (trial → paid, 20% per AGENTS.md) | 50 | 200 | 500 |
| Day 1 retention | 60% | 70% | 80% |
| Day 7 retention | 30% | 45% | 55% |
| Day 30 retention (week-5+ cohorts only) | 25% | 35% | 45% |
| Wearable connection at trial | 40% | 60% | 75% |
| Partner-invite acceptance rate | 20% | 35% | 50% |
| Press features (tier-2+) | 0 | 3 | 1 tier-1 + 5 tier-2 |
| Advisor letters returned | 1 | 3 | 5 |
| Inbound DMs/emails per week (week 8+) | 5 | 15 | 40 |

**Floor** = if we hit only these, we have a viable business and the
brand is alive. **Target** = if execution holds for 90 days. **Stretch**
= if a piece of content or an advisor breaks through.

Targets are *cumulative* unless otherwise stated. Retention is
cohort-based; only meaningful from week 5 onward.

Reached through the partner-invite mechanism, not direct marketing. The

### Tertiary — Advisors, investors, women's-health press

## Channel mix

### Owned — turn on day one

- **Email.** Forty-four templates already shipped (see
  [emails/](emails/)). The strongest brand surface. Every transactional,
  recurring, lifecycle, and celebratory moment is a touchpoint. The
  Sunday weekly review is the most-loved email; it's also the most
  shareable.
- **The product itself.** Welcome flow, today ritual, partner email.
  These are marketing.
- **Founder Instagram.** Juliana posts as Juliana, not as Vita. Vita
  signs the captions when she speaks; Juliana signs when she does. Two
  voices, one brand.

### Earned — turn on week 2

- **Founder-led PR.** One pitch per week to a named journalist. No
  spray-and-pray. Lead with the specific story, not the product.
- **Advisor outreach.** Slide 5 of the advisor deck is the template.
  Send three letters per week starting week 2. Stop sending after
  twelve unless someone replies (twelve no's is signal, not noise).
- **Studio partnerships.** Pilates and reformer studios in three
  cities. Offer free 90-day Vita to instructors in exchange for honest
  feedback. Not a referral deal — a research deal.

### Shared — turn on week 1, scale week 5

- **Instagram (primary).** Two posts per week, weeks 1–4. Three per
  week from week 5. Mix: founder POV (1/wk), Vita first-person (1/wk),
  outcome story or plan-change demo (1/wk from week 5).
- **TikTok (secondary).** One post per week from week 4. Education-led,
  not entertainment-led. "What HRV actually tells you." "Why your
  scale's up after a hard leg day." Vita reads charts in plain English.
- **LinkedIn (founder visibility).** One post every two weeks, founder
  voice. Aimed at advisor network and operator/investor audience, not
  Sarah.

### Paid — turn on day 60 only if organic content has cleared a bar

The bar: at least one organic Instagram post crosses 5,000 reach
without paid amplification, OR one piece of founder writing earns >50
inbound replies, OR one advisor publicly endorses Vita.

If the bar is met, allocate $3,000 over days 60–90 to amplify the
single best-performing organic creative. Do not run a separate paid
campaign. Boost what already worked.

If the bar is not met, do not spend on paid. Diagnose first. The brand
is not paid-ad-ready until it's organically loved.

## Success metrics — what we watch, when

### Daily (founder watches every morning, weeks 1–4 only)

- New trial starts (count, by source if attribution wired)
- Wearable connection rate on new trials
- Day 1 retention on yesterday's cohort
- Inngest job health (per LAUNCH_CHECKLIST.md after-launch step 1)

### Weekly (every Monday morning, 30 minutes)

- Cumulative trial starts vs. plan
- Trial-to-paid (% of trials past 14 days that converted)
- Sunday weekly review email open rate, reply rate, forward rate
- IG/TikTok/LinkedIn reach + saves (saves matter more than likes)
- Inbound DMs/emails (count + categorize: praise, complaint, press, advisor)

### Monthly (end of weeks 4, 8, 13)

- Day 30 retention on the cohort that just crossed it
- Partner-invite acceptance rate
- Cost-per-trial if paid is on
- One qualitative review: read three user chats end-to-end. Is the
  voice holding? Is the product earning its receipts?

### What we deliberately don't measure

- Likes, follower counts, vanity metrics. Saves and replies are
  signal; likes are noise.
- "Engagement" as an opaque metric. Either someone replies, signs up,
  refers, or they don't.
- Streaks across cohorts. AGENTS.md forbids streak shame; we don't
  internally celebrate streaks either.

## Risks & contingencies

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Apple Health webhook flakes for early users (per LAUNCH_CHECKLIST) | medium | high | Founder onboards first five personally. Watch every webhook. Phase 1 product priority is reliability, not marketing. |
| First press pitch lands flat | high | low | Plan assumes earned media is slow. Floor objectives don't require press. |
| Voice drifts on social as cadence increases | high | high | Every post run through brand/VITA-BRAND.md eight-point checklist before publishing. No exceptions, including founder posts. |
| A loud competitor (WHOOP, Apple) ships AI coaching mid-launch | medium | high | The angle Vita owns is *memory*, not AI. Memory is harder to copy. If a major competitor moves, lean harder into the compound story, not feature parity. |
| Trial-to-paid below 10% by week 6 | medium | high | Stop adding new trials. Spend the next two weeks talking to non-converters. The brief becomes "why didn't you upgrade?" not "how do we get more trials?" |
| Founder bandwidth collapses around week 6 | high | high | The plan is intentionally scoped for one person. If it still breaks, drop TikTok first, then advisor outreach, never email or product onboarding. |

## What this plan deliberately doesn't do

- **No paid ads in Phase 1 or early Phase 2.** Paid only amplifies what
  already works organically. Spending early hides whether the brand
  earns attention.
- **No influencer partnerships.** The brand voice is restrained and
  first-person. An influencer campaign breaks both.
- **No referral discounts or "$10 off" promo codes.** The partner
  mechanism is the referral system. It's built in. It doesn't need a
  bribe.
- **No drip-funnel "21-day challenge" campaign.** Every other fitness
  brand runs these. Vita's competitive position is the opposite. Don't
  run them.
- **No Twitter/X presence, no Reddit AMAs, no Product Hunt launch in
  the first 90 days.** None of these reach Sarah at quality. They
  consume founder hours that go further on Instagram + email.
- **No "we" copy anywhere, ever.** First-person Vita on every channel.
  The brand bleeds if a single channel breaks the rule.
